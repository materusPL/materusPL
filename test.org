When $a \ne 0$, there are two solutions to $(ax^2 + bx + c = 0)$ and they are

$$
\Large
\displaystyle
y(x) = 
\begin{cases} 
  \dfrac{3}{4} \cdot x^{3} \cdot \dfrac{7}{8} x & \text{dla x ∈ <-10, 0>} \\\\
  \dfrac{1}{sin(x) + 3} & \text{dla x ∈ (0, 4>} \\\\
  -2x/3  & \text{dla pozostałych} 
\end{cases}
\\
$$
